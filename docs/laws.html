Online Privacy Laws

It should be noted that no single law covers all aspects on online privacy and there are various laws that are applicable in different situations for different purposes.

Federal Laws

  • The Federal Trade Commission Act (1914) - created the FTC, which broadly regulates unfair and deceptive practices by commercial businesses, which includes regulating inadequate personal information protection.
  • Electronic Communications Privacy Act (1986) - protects spoken, wired(i.e. phone calls), and electronic communications from being used, accessed, disclosed to anybody that the owner of that information is unaware of.
  • Computer Fraud & Abuse Act (1986) - makes unauthorized access of computers to obtain certain information, take anything of value, or transmit harmful material.
  • Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (1998) - requires certain and online services to get parental permission before doing anything with a child's information and requires websites to post an online privacy policy, collect only necessary information, and have security measures that the website enforces.
  • Financial Services Modernization Act (1999) - regulates the collection and use of personal information by financial instituions, such as banks, and requires these businesses have a written information security program and that their customers be notified of these policies.
  • Fair and Accurate Credit Transactions Act (2003) - requires that financial instituions have programs to prevent identity theft.

New Jersey Laws

  • Personal Information and Privacy Protection Act (2016) - restricts the collection and use of personal information by retailers for certain purposes. This restricts the reasons a retailer may scan an identification card, such as a driver's license, and for what purposes retailers may retain your personal information. This also limits the types of information collected and forbids the sharing of any collected information outside of the company.
