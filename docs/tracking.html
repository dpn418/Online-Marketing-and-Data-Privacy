Online Tracking

Cookies

Cookies are the main way that websites track your data. Cookies are small files created by your browser and stored on your computer in order to track information when you visit different webpages. They come in two forms : session and persistent. Session cookies allow for the tracking of temporary information such as the items in an online shopping cart. Without session cookies that information would have to be re-entered every time you changed the webpage you are on. Since session cookies are temporary, these cookies are gone after you close out your web browser or log off a website. Because these cookies are not stored long term, session cookies should not be a concern for your online privacy. The second kind of cookies are persistent cookies and this type of cookie remains even after you end your browsing session. Persistent cookies are responsible for things such as targeted advertisments that are geared towards preferences that you have shown while browsing even on sites you have not visited before. This may be good or bad depending on whether or not you feel that targeted advertisements are intrusive.

Browser Fingerprinting

Browser fingerprinting is another method for a website to track your information without the use of cookies. Fingerprinting works through the use of javascript code that is sent from the webserver that hosts the webpage you are visiting and executed on your device. This javascript code can report various kinds of information, such as the type of browser you are using, your timezone, or even the hardware your machine has, back to the webserver it came from. This information that is collected adn sent back to the webserver is your digital fingerprint. If unique information about you is collected, then your activity can be tracked by that website or any other website that the webserver shares your unique fingerprint information with.
