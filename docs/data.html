Surveillence Capitalism

What kinds of data do companies collect?

When you visit different websites like Yoututbe or use search engines like Google, much more data about you is collected than you could imagine. Beyond the basic information like your name or your phone number that you enter when you create an account online, companies also collect copious amounts of mundane information like what links you click or what your favorite genre of music by tracking you and your activity. Every small interaction you have with a website can be tracked by the big tech companies that own the website.

What is being done with your data?

This massive amount of information that most people do not even realize that they are inadvertantly giving away to these big companies are being used to build profiles about people and fuel algorithms that these companies make profit off of. Under the guise of "improving services", these companies collect, use, and profit off of your information. By building predictive models off of all this data and grouping you by what preferences you have shown, tech companies are able quite accurately guess things like what kind of food you like or what products or services you are most likely to buy. Using these models, helps companies like Google earn money from other companies by targeting you with advertisements or providing links to products or services that you have the highest predictive chances to spend your money on.
